Visualize the Lorentz Force and understand its impact on charged particles.

The Physics Behind the Simulator

The motion of a charged particle in a magnetic field is governed by the Lorentz Force, given by the equation:

F = q(E + v × B)

Since we are assuming no electric field (E = 0), the equation simplifies to:

F = q(v × B)

In this simulation, q represents the charge of the particle in Coulombs, v represents the velocity of the particle in meters per second (m/s), and B represents the magnetic field in Tesla.

The Lorentz Force is always perpendicular to the particle's velocity, causing the particle to follow a circular path. The radius of the circular motion depends on the particle's speed and the magnetic field strength. It is given by:

r = (m × v) / (q × B)

Here, m represents the mass of the particle.

Simulation Key:

The yellow dot represents the charged particle affected by the Lorentz Force. It moves in a circular path determined by the force, and its position is dynamically updated based on the equations of motion.

The red line represents the instantaneous velocity of the particle. The line is tangent to the circular path, and its direction changes continuously as the particle moves, reflecting the constant change in velocity direction due to the magnetic force.
